Services

LifeBridge Health’s orthopedic surgeons are devoted to the prevention, diagnosis and treatment of disorders of the bones, joints, ligaments, tendons and muscles. Our bone, joint and pain doctors specialize in general orthopedic care, diabetic limb preservation, foot and ankle surgery, hand and upper extremity surgery, joint preservation and replacement (including total and partial hip and knee replacement), sports medicine care and more; and can effectively treat conditions, such as:

  • Arthritis and Rheumatoid Arthritis -- swelling and tenderness of one or more of your joints 
  • Bone Deformities and Infections 
  • Carpal Tunnel and Tarsal Tunnel -- caused by increased pressure on a nerve entering the hand (carpal) or foot (tarsal). 
  • Charcot Foot -- condition causing weakening of the bones in the foot that can occur in people who have significant nerve damage 
  • Chronic Knee Joint Pain 
  • Chronic Orthopedic Hip Pain  
  • Diabetic and Non-Healing Wounds 
  • Fractures and Dislocations 
  • General Orthopedic and Musculoskeletal Conditions 
  • Gout -- a common and complex form of arthritis 
  • Pseudogout -- gout-like attacks, characterized by acute localized pain and swelling, that occur in patients with calcium crystal deposits in the joints 
  • Joint Pain and Injuries 
  • Limb Length Discrepancy and Deformity
  • Lymphedema -- Swelling in one arm or leg 
  • Neuropathic Ulcers -- form because of peripheral neuropathy, typically in diabetic patients
  • Orthopedic Trauma -- any severe injury to the bones, joints, or soft tissue
  • Orthopedic Tumors -- an abnormal growth of cells within the bone 
  • Osteoarthritis -- occurs when the protective cartilage that cushions the ends of your bones wears down over time. Although osteoarthritis can damage any joint, the disorder most commonly affects joints in your hands, knees, hips and spine
  • Osteomyelitis -- a bone infection that can cause inflammation and swelling the bone
  • Osteonecrosis -- a disease caused by reduced blood flow to bones in the joints
  • Peripheral Vascular Disease (PVD) -- a blood circulation disorder that causes the blood vessels outside of your heart and brain to narrow, block or spasm. PVD often causes pain and fatigue in the legs 
  • Sports Injuries 
  • Scoliosis -- sideways curvature of the spine
