The Sinai Difference: Leaders Learn Here

Regardless of whether you want to go into academia or pursue private practice, our graduates consistently feel more than adequately prepared for independent patient management. Here are a few reasons why.


A Resident-Focused Program
With no competing fellows in your program, you’ll get more hands-on opportunities and more individualized attention from the beginning—and achieve a higher level of autonomy faster.

Broader Clinical Exposure
Sinai offers a wider continuum of patient care services in-house, giving you in-depth experience managing patients for the entirety of their hospital stay.

Diverse Patient Population
As Maryland’s largest community teaching hospital, Sinai offers unparalleled diversity in patient demographics, including globe-spanning immigrant populations.

Johns Hopkins Partnership
You’ll complete core and elective sub-specialty rotations with Johns Hopkins, giving you world-leading experience in areas like oncology and transplants.

Next-Level Academic Support
Prepare for post-residency success from the very beginning with our longitudinal board preparation program. We also offer technology stipends, research stipends, and scholarship support.

Cutting-Edge Technology
Our state-of-the-art training initiatives, including our Simulation Lab and point of care ultrasound training, give you the skills and confidence to become a leader.

A Curriculum Built For Your Success

Sinai Hospital's internal medicine residency program provides you with a three-year curriculum, emphasizing the skills required to practice general internal medicine and the proficiency needed for board certification. Our curriculum covers all subspecialty areas of medicine as well as topics related to medicine, nutrition, ethics and research, among other areas.
