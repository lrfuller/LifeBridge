Improving the well-being of the community

Northwest Hospital, a nonprofit hospital with 231 beds, has served the northwest Baltimore community for more than 50 years. With a patient-friendly design and impressive array of specialties, the hospital provides patient-centered care with an emphasis on quality and safety.

Going beyond the expected, Northwest has recently earned top honors for several innovative safety initiatives. To bring care into the community, Northwest has built nearby facilities that offer convenient services such as outpatient surgery, adult day care and physical rehabilitation.

Our Story
Established in 1962 as Liberty Court Rehabilitation Center, and in 1963 became Baltimore County General Hospital. In June of 1993, it changed its name to Northwest Hospital.

Patient and Visitor Information

Whether you’re a patient at Northwest Hospital or a first-time visitor, our staff is committed to providing you high quality, personalized and professional care.

The purpose of this patient/visitor portal is to answer your questions about the hospital and our services. This portal will provide all the information you need for your visit with us. If you are a patient, you can learn about preparing for your hospitalization, meeting your caregivers and following up after discharge. Visitors and family members can find directions to the hospital, a campus map and important phone numbers.

We know that a hospital visit can be a stressful time for patients and family members, so our goal is to make your stay with us as pleasant as possible.

Thank you for choosing Northwest Hospital for your medical needs.

Need Help Paying Your Bill?

Thank you for choosing Northwest Hospital as your healthcare provider. Our Patient Financial Services Department is available to assist patients who do not carry medical insurance (uninsured) or face significant co-payment, coinsurance and/or deductible charges, which may be challenging to manage due to personal hardship or financial distress. Depending on the specific financial situation, a patient may be eligible to receive Maryland Medical Assistance (Medicaid), Financial Assistance or take advantage of extended payment plans.
