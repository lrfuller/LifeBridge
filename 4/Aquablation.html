How Does It Work?

Aquablation therapy uses the power of water delivered with robotic precision to provide long-lasting BPH relief without compromise. It is precise, consistent and predictable, and provides long-term relief no matter how large your prostate.

This two-step procedure includes:

  1. Creating a surgical map
    • Aquablation is the only BPH procedure that integrates ultrasound imaging with a cystoscope. This gives the surgeon the ability to see the entire prostate in real time, allowing them to map which parts of the prostate to remove and avoid. This allows the surgeon to avoid removing parts of the prostate that can cause irreversible complications.
  2. Removing prostate tissue
    • A robotically controlled, heat-free waterjet removes the prostate tissue that has been mapped out. This robotic technology minimizes human error in removing prostate tissue, and ensures the prostate tissue is removed precisely, consistently and predictably.

Who Should Consider Aquablation?

Men experiencing symptoms relating to their ability to hold urine or empty the bladder may qualify as a candidate for this therapy. Symptoms include:

  • Ability to hold urine
    • Urgency: the need to urinate immediately or urgently
    • Frequency: the need to urinate more often than normal
    • Nocturia: the need to urinate frequently while sleeping
    • Incontinence: complete loss of the ability to hold urine
  • Emptying the bladder
    • Straining: the need to push to start or complete urinating
    • Dysuria: pain or stinging while urinating
    • Hesitancy: difficulty starting to urinate
    • Weak stream: a gentle instead of strong stream of urine
    • Intermittency: a stream that starts and stops
    • Retention: complete loss of the ability to empty the bladder
